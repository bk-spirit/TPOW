What we're building toward

A world where work serves life—where Monday mornings feel like possibility, not punishment. Where organizations operate from wholeness instead of extraction. Where your daily activities contribute to solutions rather than perpetuating problems.

Our core principles:

  • Meeting situations deeply rather than matching predetermined solutions
  • Gift-based economics that create connection rather than ending it
  • Transcontextual learning across different domains and ways of knowing
  • Emergence over engineering - trusting organic intelligence
  • No one turned away for lack of funds

What we're growing into:

  • More accessible pathways for people with financial constraints
  • Deeper racial and class diversity in our network
  • Rural and working-class perspectives on regenerative economics
  • Institutional partnerships through ISI fiscal sponsorship
  • Communities of practice in education, healthcare, technology

The Real Questions

Is this actually working?

Honestly? This is generational work. We're building alternatives to extractive systems through gift economy principles and emergence-based approaches. Some people find deep community and livelihood. Others try it and move on.

What works: People who commit to 6+ months of consistent participation often experience real transformation in their relationship with work and community.

What doesn't: We're still learning how to serve people with serious financial constraints or those from very different cultural backgrounds.

The money question

Real talk: We operate on gift economy principles - dhana (contribute what feels right), needs-based scholarships, work-trade options, and standard pricing for those with capacity.

Current structure: Educational work is fiscally sponsored through Inquiring Systems Institute. Consulting work happens through CrowdWork cooperative to support the commons.

Support available: No one turned away for lack of funds. We're actively exploring how to better serve people with serious financial obligations.

What we're actually offering

Community Learning: Monthly CommDojo (communication), Conflict Kinship Dojo, Soul Space, and other practice spaces using gift economy.

Deep Explorations: ATEM: Reimagining Philanthropy lab (6-month warm data process with diverse stakeholders) - $200 with extensive scholarships.

Individual Support: Journey coaching, organizational consulting through CrowdWork, Work Tech/Power Design for human-AI relations.

Who this isn't for

People looking for quick networking, guaranteed income streams, or traditional business solutions. This is about fundamental transformation of our relationship with work, economics, and community - which takes time and commitment.

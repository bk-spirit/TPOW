What we're building toward

A world where work serves life—where Monday mornings feel like possibility, not punishment. Where organizations operate from wholeness instead of extraction. Where your job contributes to solutions rather than perpetuating problems.

What we will not compromise on:

  • Humans are whole beings, not resources to optimize
  • Real change takes time—we're not selling quick fixes
  • Both inner work and systems change are necessary
  • Community accountability over individual advancement
  • Planetary wellbeing is not optional

What we're growing into:

  • More accessible pathways for financial transition
  • Deeper racial and class diversity in our community
  • Rural and blue-collar perspectives on regenerative work
  • Policy influence at municipal and state levels
  • 1000+ organizations experimenting by 2030

The Real Questions

Is this actually working?

Honestly? About 30% of people stick around long-term. Some experiments fail. Changing work culture is generational work.

What works: Organizations committing 6+ months see real culture shifts. We've helped launch 12 cooperatives.

What doesn't: We're mostly urban, educated, tech-adjacent. We struggle to serve people with serious financial constraints.

The money question

Real talk: Most people have financial privilege - savings, partners with steady income, or freelancing skills.

Guild model: 40 people earn $60-80k annually through our professional cooperative doing consulting work.

Transition support: $200k mutual aid fund supporting 50+ career transitions over 6-18 months.

Who this isn't for

People looking for quick networking, guaranteed income, or who can't commit to 3+ months of consistent participation.

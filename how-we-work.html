How We Work

Understanding our ecosystem structure, learning approach, and governance

Our Ecosystem Structure

Think of TPOW like a forest ecosystem—each layer enables what grows above it. Like in a forest, each layer feeds and is fed by the others, creating a living cycle that makes the whole ecosystem more resilient and abundant.

Commons Level - The Soil

Like rich forest soil, this is where all the nutrients, relationships, and foundational learning happen. Monthly gatherings, open conversations, shared resources, and collaborative research create the fertile ground where everything else grows. Just as healthy soil supports all plant life, our commons supports all other activities.

Platform & Network Level - The Root System

Like the vast underground network of tree roots and mycorrhizal fungi, this invisible infrastructure connects everything and shares resources. Our "Present of X" initiatives, project tracks like ATEM, and social protocols help people find each other and self-organize around shared interests.

Containers Level - The Trees and Plants

Like different species of trees that create distinct micro-environments, these are specific spaces where people can practice and experiment. Starter Cultures, intensive retreats, and workshop series each create their own "climate" for particular kinds of growth.

Applications Level - The Fruits and Seeds

Like fruits and seeds that nourish other creatures and spread to create new forests, these are practical offerings that apply our commons wisdom in the wider world. Consulting, training, and transformation projects carry our DNA into new environments.

The magic: Like in a forest, each layer feeds and is fed by the others. Wisdom flows up from commons through containers to applications, while resources and new learning flow back down, creating a living cycle that makes the whole ecosystem more resilient and abundant.

Four Operational Directions

Our ecosystem flows through four operational directions that provide organic structure for daily activities. Understanding these helps you see where your interests and skills might fit.

Content & Communication

How we share insights and invite participation through Substack publications, YouTube content, and storytelling initiatives. If you're a writer, podcaster, researcher, or love exploring ideas, this is where you'd engage.

Research & Experimentation

Active explorations and collaborative investigations through Projects, Tracks, and Interest groups. If you want to dive deep into a particular area with others, you'd join one of these tracks.

Business Applications

Organizations integrating our frameworks into their operations through consulting, training, and transformation projects. If you're part of an organization wanting to transform how it operates, this is your entry point.

Network Facilitation

People who connect others and hold space for emergence. If you're naturally good at seeing connections and facilitating relationships, this could be your role.

Four Pathways for Exploration

These pathways are interconnected ways of engaging that can deepen your capacity for life-affirming work. Think of them as a spiral—you can step in wherever feels most aligned, and they naturally build on each other over time.

How the pathways work together:

You might start by exploring ideas, which leads to joining a project, which inspires you to transform your organization, which develops your skills as a connector. Or you might begin by weaving connections, which helps you see organizational needs, which motivates you to explore the frameworks more deeply.

Explore Ideas

Follow content, attend gatherings, experiment with the five-domain framework in your life. Develops your conceptual understanding and personal practice.

Join Projects

Participate in ongoing explorations like alternative economics, relational organizing, or regenerative business practices. Builds practical experience and collaborative skills.

Transform Organizations

Bring approaches to your workplace through consulting, training, or internal initiatives. Develops your capacity to create systemic change.

Weave Connections

Help others find their place and facilitate relationships between people and projects. Strengthens your network weaving and facilitation abilities.

Learning & Governance

If you're considering getting involved, you probably want to know how decisions get made and how you'd develop your capabilities within our ecosystem.

How We Define Learning

Drawing from Nora Bateson's work on "warm data" and mutual learning, we understand learning as the continuous process of adjusting relationships within living systems. Rather than downloading information, learning happens through participating in the patterns that connect—between people, ideas, and contexts.

Our Method: Learning Through Doing Together

Instead of traditional instruction, we use collaborative practice. This looks like co-hosting gatherings where you develop facilitation skills in real-time, participating in organizational transformation projects where you learn systems thinking by engaging with actual systems, or joining research cohorts where you develop expertise through collaborative inquiry.

Play and Experimentation

We approach learning playfully—trying things, noticing what happens, adjusting, and trying again. For instance, a "Present of Education" group might experiment with different meeting formats each month, reflecting together on what emerges, and gradually developing new ways of learning that serve both individual growth and collective wisdom.

Governance as Collective Learning

Decisions emerge through distributed leadership and collective sense-making rather than top-down control. Governance becomes a form of mutual learning—sensing what wants to emerge through conversations in monthly gatherings, collaborative research in project tracks, or coordinated experiments across multiple communities.

For Different Types of Engagement

Organizational Leaders & Hiring Authorities

Your teams can develop real collaboration skills through our approach, not just attend workshops. We offer organizational transformation support that builds internal capacity while achieving business outcomes. Many leaders find this reduces turnover, improves decision-making quality, and increases innovation.

Funders & Donors

Supporting TPOW means investing in systemic change rather than temporary fixes. Your funding helps create replicable models and shared resources that amplify impact across multiple organizations and communities. We offer transparent reporting on both measurable outcomes and emergent learning.

Individual Contributors

Whether you're contributing time, skills, or presence, you'll develop capabilities that serve both your personal growth and collective impact. Many participants report increased sense of purpose, stronger professional relationships, and enhanced capacity to navigate complexity.

Network Connectors

If you're good at seeing patterns and facilitating relationships, you'll find opportunities to strengthen entire ecosystems of change-makers while developing your own practice of systems thinking and emergence.

Our Metawork Framework

We see work not as jobs or careers, but as the fundamental human activity of creating, contributing, and participating in the world. Our framework engages five domains of human experience:

The Five Domains:

  • Relationships: Nurturing authentic connection, care, and co-creation
  • Meaning: Aligning efforts with deepest values and purpose
  • Mastery: Developing unique gifts and capacities in service of impact
  • Energy: Sustaining vitality, presence, and wellbeing in work
  • Perspective: Opening to multiple ways of knowing, being, and creating

For example, instead of networking for personal gain, you might practice authentic vulnerability in team meetings (Relationships). Rather than climbing a predetermined career ladder, you might explore how your values can shape the projects you choose (Meaning). Instead of pushing through burnout, you might experiment with sustainable rhythms (Energy).

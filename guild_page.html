What is a Guild?

Historically, guilds were associations of skilled craftspeople and merchants in medieval and Renaissance Europe who maintained quality standards, supported member development, protected trade interests, and shared knowledge for the common good. Guilds ensured that expertise was passed down through apprenticeships, that practitioners maintained ethical standards, and that the broader community benefited from their skills.

Participation Pathways

We recognize that people have both contribution desires and livelihood needs. We've intentionally created multiple pathways so that meaningful participation isn't limited by financial circumstances, while also offering opportunities for people to create sustainable income without compromising their integrity.

Commons Participation

Volunteer-led engagement through our Open Collective, supported by donations and community contributions.

For you if: You want to contribute time and energy without financial exchange, support the broader movement, or are exploring while between other opportunities.

Skill Sharing

Pro-bono contribution of professional expertise through our Open Source Commons.

For you if: You want to contribute your capabilities (design, facilitation, writing, etc.) to community projects while maintaining other income sources.

Project Development

Individual initiatives that can be funded through investments, sponsorships, grants, or community support (including dhana/gift-based funding).

For you if: You have a specific project idea and want to develop it into potentially income-generating work, or want to join funded initiatives.

Fiscal Sponsorship

Institutional support for experimental projects through sponsors like ISI (grants) and RAFT (experimental forms and community building).

For you if: You have an innovative project that needs organizational backing and institutional credibility.

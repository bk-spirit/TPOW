Meta Guilds

Learning communities that operate in the liminal spaces between skill development, spiritual practice, and systems transformation

What Makes Meta Guilds Different

Unlike traditional guilds that focus on specific trades or even modern learning programs that separate skill development from personal growth, meta guilds recognize that in the present of work, developing capacity and developing consciousness are inseparable processes.

Meta Guilds: Beyond Traditional Models

We've evolved beyond traditional guild structures to create "meta guilds" - learning communities that operate in the liminal spaces between existing frameworks and emerging possibilities.

Traditional Guild Limitations

Historical guilds served important functions but had significant constraints:

  • Exclusionary membership based on access and privilege
  • Rigid hierarchies that limited innovation
  • Protectionist attitudes that hoarded knowledge
  • Market-based thinking that prioritized profit over purpose
  • Separation of work from spiritual and relational development

The Meta Guild Approach

Our meta guilds operate from different principles entirely:

  • Inclusive access through gift economy and scholarship principles
  • Emergence-based learning that honors diverse wisdom traditions
  • Open-source knowledge sharing that strengthens the commons
  • Integration of work as presence, present moment, and gift
  • Recognition that skill development and soul development are inseparable

Operating in the Liminal Web

Meta guilds exist in the spaces between established categories:

  • Between education and spiritual practice
  • Between individual development and collective transformation
  • Between ancient wisdom and contemporary innovation
  • Between livelihood and sacred work
  • Between local community and global networks

The Present of Work Framework

Meta guilds are designed around our three-layer learning ecosystem where participants simultaneously:

• Contribute to the Soil/Commons: Share learning and resources that nourish the whole network

• Develop as Knots: Strengthen their unique position in webs of relationship

• Participate in the Social Web: Apply learning to real-world transformation projects

This integration means skill development becomes inseparable from community building and systems change.

Guild Formation & Governance

Meta guilds emerge from community needs and practitioner readiness rather than predetermined curricula. Our governance structure ensures both quality and accessibility.

1

Community Sensing

Guild topics emerge from patterns we notice in community calls, dojo participation, and requests for deeper exploration of specific themes.

2

Practitioner Readiness

Experienced community members who've developed expertise through commons participation can propose facilitating a guild in their area of strength.

3

Capacity Assessment

We evaluate: facilitator experience, community demand, resource requirements, integration with existing offerings, and sustainability for facilitators.

4

Commons Approval

Guild proposals are discussed in monthly community calls using consent-based decision making. Community input shapes format, timing, and accessibility.

Current Capacity & Selection Criteria

• Guild Capacity: 2-3 active guilds at any time to maintain quality and community integration

• Facilitator Support: Co-facilitation model with experienced mentors and emerging facilitators

• Community Need: Minimum 6-8 committed participants expressing genuine interest

• Resource Alignment: Sustainable for facilitators while maintaining accessibility principles

• Ecosystem Integration: Connects to and strengthens our three-layer learning ecosystem

Types of Guild Offerings

• Labs at Cost: Warm Data Guild - minimal fees to cover space/food or prevent no-shows, with option to contribute to commons

• Skill Development Guilds: Standard gift economy pricing ($75-150/month) with extensive scholarships

• Professional Development Guilds: For experienced practitioners wanting to offer services

• Experimental Guilds: Pilot programs testing new approaches with community feedback

Participant Journey Through Meta Guilds

1

Community Connection

Start with CommDojo, community calls, or dojos to understand our approach and build relationships before applying to intensive guilds.

2

Guild Participation

Engage in intensive learning combining theory with practice, peer coaching, and real-world application with mentor guidance.

3

Integration & Application

Apply learning through community projects, potentially co-facilitate future guilds, and develop professional practice if desired.

4

Ongoing Community

Remain connected through the three-layer ecosystem, contributing to commons while developing your unique gifts and capacities.

Guild Principles

• Gift Economy: Multiple pricing levels based on capacity, extensive scholarships, work-trade options

• Peer Learning: Everyone teaches and learns - no expert/student hierarchy

• Real Application: Practice with actual challenges, not just theoretical exercises

• Relationship Building: Focus on lasting connections and ongoing mutual support

• Commons Integration: Learning flows back to strengthen the shared knowledge base

What to Expect from Meta Guild Participation

Clear expectations help ensure guilds meet your actual needs rather than creating unrealistic hopes

What Guilds Will Provide

Deep skill development through intensive practice with experienced mentors and committed peers over 8-12 weeks.

Integration of personal development with practical capacity building - you can't separate skill from soul.

Lasting professional relationships and ongoing support network within our ecosystem.

Real-world application opportunities with community organizations needing your emerging skills.

Foundation for developing consulting, coaching, or facilitation practice if that's your path.

What Needs Guilds Meet

Need for meaningful community and peer learning relationships that support both growth and accountability.

Desire to develop expertise in approaches that integrate values with practical effectiveness.

Longing to contribute your gifts while also receiving mentorship and skill development.

Want to explore livelihood pathways that align with your values rather than compromising integrity.

Need for ongoing support in navigating complex organizational and community challenges.

What Guilds Won't Guarantee

Immediate income or guaranteed job placement - livelihood development depends on your existing skills and local opportunities.

Quick personal transformation if you're not willing to practice consistently and receive feedback openly.

Perfect work-life integration or resolution of all career challenges - systemic issues require systemic change.

Answers to all questions about your life direction - guilds provide community and skills, not life coaching.

Professional certification or credentials recognized by traditional institutions.

Who Guilds Work Best For

• People with 6-10 hours per week to commit to learning and practice over 8-12 weeks

• Those who learn best through community engagement rather than solo study

• Practitioners wanting to integrate personal development with skill building

• People interested in long-term community engagement, not quick skill acquisition

• Those willing to give and receive feedback in supportive peer relationships

Financial Reality & Accessibility

Being transparent about costs and our commitment to ensuring financial barriers don't prevent participation

Gift Economy Pricing

Most guilds operate on gift economy principles with multiple access levels:

Standard Contribution: $75-150/month based on guild intensity and duration

Scholarship Support: Reduced or waived fees based on financial need - no one turned away

Work-Trade: Contribute skills (documentation, design, social media) in exchange for participation

At-Cost Labs: Warm Data and similar offerings charged only for space/food costs

Sustainability for Facilitators

We balance accessibility with fair compensation for facilitators:

Guild facilitators receive support through a combination of participant contributions, commons fund subsidies, and consulting revenue sharing.

No facilitator should bear financial burden for making guilds accessible to those with financial constraints.

Part of guild fees support scholarships for future participants, creating a regenerative cycle.

Value Beyond Cost

Guild participation provides value that extends far beyond the initial time commitment:

Ongoing access to community support network and professional referrals for years after completion.

Potential pathways to consulting work through CrowdWork or independent practice development.

Skills and relationships that continue developing through community engagement and peer mentorship.

Foundation for contributing as facilitator or mentor in future guild offerings.
